## Key Findings

### 1) BERT-base is the strongest overall candidate

Across available multiseed experiments, bert_base_baseline is the best performer on both mean accuracy and mean macro-F1.

### 2) BERTweet slang_masked is unstable and likely problematic

The bertweet_slang_masked multiseed result is an outlier:

- Accuracy mean: 0.5773
- Accuracy std: 0.2958
- Macro-F1 mean: 0.4313
- Macro-F1 std: 0.4133

This variance is much larger than other experiments and suggests severe run instability or failure modes for this setting.

### 3) Slang-heavy-only BERT finetuning hurt performance

Using apples-to-apples slang-heavy comparisons:

- Baseline original on slang-heavy subgroup vs bert_finetune_slang_heavy
  - Accuracy delta (finetune - baseline): -0.0370
  - Macro-F1 delta (finetune - baseline): -0.0375

- Baseline mixed on slang-heavy subgroup vs bert_finetune_slang_heavy_mixed
  - Accuracy delta (finetune - baseline): -0.0308
  - Macro-F1 delta (finetune - baseline): -0.0312

These deltas indicate consistent degradation after slang-heavy-only finetuning.

## Statistical Significance Checks

Paired tests on seed 42 (matched by base_id) confirm the degradation is statistically significant:

1. baseline vs bert_finetune_slang_heavy

- File: outputs/modeling/paired_significance_bert_vs_finetune_slang_seed42.json
- Accuracy difference (finetune - baseline): -0.0340
- McNemar p-value: 1.53e-09
- Sign test p-value: 6.55e-10
- Bootstrap 95% CI for accuracy diff: [-0.0445, -0.0230]

2. baseline mixed vs bert_finetune_slang_heavy_mixed

- File: outputs/modeling/paired_significance_bertmixed_vs_finetune_mixed_seed42.json
- Accuracy difference (finetune - baseline): -0.0266
- McNemar p-value: 7.20e-08
- Sign test p-value: 3.58e-08
- Bootstrap 95% CI for accuracy diff: [-0.0367, -0.0170]

Conclusion: the finetuned variants are significantly worse than the corresponding baselines in these tests.
